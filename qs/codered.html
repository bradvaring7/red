📝 CODE RED PROTOCOLS

🚨 "Code Red" scenarios are considered serious or highly escalated customer situations. Whenever we encounter any of these scenarios with a customer, we should promptly fill out the Code Red Form to ensure it is appropriately addressed and follow the correct steps for resolution.

📝 Steps to Submit a Code Red

🖱️ Click on the link and fill out the Code Red Form

🖊️ Fill out the fields:

📝 Brief Summary of Situation

Provide a concise description of the customer’s issue, including key points of the conversation and any relevant background

🛠️ De-escalation Methods/Offers Used

Outline steps taken to de-escalate the situation, including any offers, accommodations, or solutions presented to the customer. If no solutions were provided, explain why

📞 Interaction End Result

Describe how the call concluded. Did the customer accept or reject the offers? Were they satisfied or still upset? Were they advised that a supervisor would follow up?

👨‍💼 Supervisor Transfer

If the call was transferred to a supervisor, mention this in the submission and provide the supervisor’s name

📇 Contact Information for Non-Account Holders

If a non-account holder (spouse, sibling) initiated the call and it escalated into a Code Red, ensure their phone number is included in the submission

📧 Role of the Executive Resolution Specialist

  • 📬 Handles each email and ensures it is appropriately addressed
  • 🔗 Ties any loose ends that could cause further escalation
  • 📅 Ensures follow-up with the customer within 24 hours

🌟 Benefits of Prompt Resolution

  • 😊 Increases customer retention: Customers are more likely to remain with Greenix when their problems are resolved quickly
  • ⚖️ Reduces legal risks: Minimizes the risk of legal action or serious consequences from unresolved issues

📋 Examples of Code Red Scenarios

  • 📰 Media Outlets, Law Enforcement, Lawyers, Legal Officials: Submit to Code Red and notify supervisor/cc in email
  • 📞 Non-Customer Calling in with Employee Accusation Claims: Collect information and let them know the appropriate department will call back within 24-48 business hours
  • 💬 News/Social Media Threats: Customer threatens to post on news, social media, or leave a bad review
  • ⚖️ Attorney General: Customer threatens to contact the attorney general
  • 🏢 Better Business Bureau: Customer threatens to contact the BBB
  • ⚖️ Customer Has a Lawyer: Customer threatens to get a lawyer or has a lawyer, threatening to sue
  • 🏠 Severe Home/Pet Damage: Service professional caused $5,000+ damage to a customer's home or suspected pet injuries from the product
  • 🐜 Infestations/Persisting Unresolved Pest Issue: Schedule a VIP appointment on a Lead Service Pro to assess and resolve the issue
